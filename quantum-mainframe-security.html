Quantum Threats to Mainframe Cryptography: Anticipating Post-Quantum Risks in IBM Z Security Architecture

Author: Yiqi Gu
Published: 2024
Affiliation: IBM Expert Lab, Australia

The convergence of quantum computing research and enterprise cryptographic infrastructure presents a fundamental question: What happens when current encryption can no longer resist decryption?

IBM Z mainframes currently leverage FIPS 140-2 certified cryptographic hardware, built-in CPACF acceleration, and pervasive encryption to deliver industry-leading data protection. However, most of these algorithms — RSA, ECC, even certain AES modes — rest on assumptions that quantum algorithms (e.g., Shor’s, Grover’s) could shatter.

Key considerations now emerging in IBM Z and DevSecOps contexts include:

  • How RACF key management integrates with NIST post-quantum candidates
  • Impact on digital signature trust chains (X.509, ICSF keys)
  • Quantum-safe TLS integration in z/OS communication stacks
  • Migration timelines before quantum decryption becomes real

Quantum computers strong enough to pose decryption threats may still be 5–15 years away. But IBM’s dual role — as both a pioneer of quantum computing and the steward of the world’s most secure classical architecture — creates a unique lens on the future.

This paper outlines an engineer’s perspective on mapping today's mainframe encryption structures toward a post-quantum posture, long before the adversary arrives.
